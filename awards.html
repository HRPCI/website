Awards & Recognitions

The Human Rights Protection Council, India (HRPCI) confers national-level awards and honorary recognitions to acknowledge individuals and institutions who have demonstrated outstanding commitment to social service, education, human rights, public welfare, and nation-building.

These awards are conferred to honor integrity, dedication, ethical leadership, and long-term contribution to society. HRPCI awards are purely honorary and social recognitions.

Purpose of HRPCI Awards

  • To recognize exceptional service and social contribution
  • To encourage ethical leadership and responsible citizenship
  • To highlight positive role models in society
  • To inspire others toward community service and nation-building

Nomination Process

  • Nominations may be submitted by individuals, institutions, or HRPCI members
  • Self-nomination may be permitted where applicable
  • Nomination must include contribution details and supporting documents
  • Submission does not guarantee selection

Selection Process

  • Preliminary screening of nominations by HRPCI Secretariat
  • Evaluation by an internal selection or advisory committee
  • Assessment based on merit, contribution, impact, and ethics
  • Final approval by the authorized governing body of HRPCI

Recognition & Felicitation

  • Formal felicitation during seminar, conference, or award ceremony
  • Presentation of award, citation, and certificate
  • Optional media coverage and organizational acknowledgment

Fees & Transparency Policy

Any fees, if applicable, are strictly towards:

  • Administrative processing and documentation
  • Event organization and venue arrangements
  • Ceremonial materials, certificates, trophies, and logistics

Important: HRPCI does not sell awards or degrees. Payment of fees does not guarantee selection. All recognitions are conferred based on merit and committee approval.

Ethical Standards & Withdrawal Policy

HRPCI reserves the right to withdraw any award or recognition if:

  • False or misleading information is discovered
  • The award is misused for illegal or unethical purposes
  • The recipient brings disrepute to the organization or society

Final Disclaimer

All awards and recognitions conferred by Human Rights Protection Council, India (HRPCI) are honorary in nature. They do not imply governmental, statutory, or academic authority and should be represented truthfully and ethically.
